Phase 1 — Days 1 to 30 — Stop the bleeding. Generate cash now.
1
Launch paid afternoon daycare immediately
Announce extended care 12–5pm to all current parents via WhatsApp this week. Price at ₹1,500/month per child. Your staff is already present. Your space is already paid for. This is pure margin — zero additional fixed cost required.
+₹22,500–37,500 / branch / month
2
Activate a structured parent referral program
Offer existing parents a ₹500–1,000 fee discount for every confirmed new admission they refer. Print referral cards. Brief every staff member to mention it weekly. You have 27 parents per branch — that is 27 potential salespeople who already trust you.
Each new student = ₹3,000/month recurring. Cost: ₹500–1,000 once.
3
Create a WhatsApp broadcast list per branch today
Set up WhatsApp Business for each branch principal. Upload all parent numbers. Post 3 times a week: one child activity photo, one parenting tip, one branch update. Cost is zero and builds steady top-of-mind awareness in your locality within weeks.
2–4 new enquiries per week per branch — ₹0 cost
Phase 2 — Days 31 to 90 — Fill the seats. Fix the cost structure.
4
Launch hyper-local digital admission campaigns
Spend ₹5,000/month per branch on Facebook and Instagram. Geo-target within 3km radius. Target mothers aged 22–35. Run a "Free trial day" offer. At your fee level, a single converted admission pays for two months of ads. This is the lowest-cost lead engine available to you.
₹5,000 ad spend → 3–5 new admissions → ₹9,000–15,000 recurring/month
5
Implement hub-and-spoke staffing model
Keep one core teacher and one helper at each branch as permanent staff. Hire 2–3 specialist teachers (music, art, phonics) as shared floating staff who rotate across branches. This reduces monthly salary costs by 15–25% while actually improving curriculum quality — specialists become a selling point.
Saves ₹20,000–40,000 / month across the network
6
Reprice with a 3-tier fee structure
Introduce: Basic ₹2,500 | Standard ₹3,500 | Premium ₹4,500 (includes activity kit and monthly progress report). Research consistently shows 60–70% of parents choose the middle or top tier. This lifts average revenue per student by ₹500–1,000 without losing enrollments.
+₹500–1,000 avg per student = +₹13,500–27,000 / branch / month
Phase 3 — Month 3 to 6 — Build recurring revenue. Eliminate dry spells.
7
Launch an annual summer camp open to all neighbourhood children
Run a 4-week structured program in April–May with themes: art week, science week, performance week, sports week. Price at ₹3,500–5,000 per child. Open registration to non-enrolled children in the locality — this is your single biggest annual marketing event AND your best profit month.
20 kids × ₹4,000 × 8 branches = ₹6,40,000 in one month
8
Sell weekend hobby classes as a standalone product
Saturday art, dance, or phonics batches for children aged 2–7 — open to non-enrolled children in the locality. Charge ₹800–1,200/month for 4 Saturday sessions. Your space is idle on weekends. Run two batches of 10 children per branch.
2 batches × ₹10,000 × 8 branches = ₹1,60,000 / month additional
9
Introduce annual enrollment fee + branded activity kit
Charge a one-time ₹1,500–2,000 registration fee at the start of each academic year. Bundle a branded activity kit (costs ₹200 to assemble, perceived value ₹800+) that includes a colouring book, crayons, name tag, and school bag tag. This creates a healthy upfront cash injection every June–July.
27 students × ₹1,800 × 8 branches = ₹3,88,800 lump sum / year
